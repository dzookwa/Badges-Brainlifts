  #### **1\. Interpersonal Influences on Adolescent Materialism: A New Look at the Role of Parents and Peers**

**Authors:** Lan Nguyen Chaplin & Deborah Roedder John (2010)  
 **Journal:** Journal of Consumer Psychology, 20(2), 176-184

**Abstract:** What causes adolescents to be materialistic? This study examines how parents and peers influence adolescent materialism, proposing that supportive parents and peers boost adolescents' self-esteem, which decreases their need to turn to material goods. The research with 12–18 year-olds finds that self-esteem mediates the relationship between parent/peer influence and adolescent materialism. Both parental support and peer support inhibit materialism, while parents' and peers' own materialism encourage it in adolescents.

**PDF Available:** [Direct PDF Link](http://assets.csom.umn.edu/assets/166371.pdf) | [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/228464766_Interpersonal_Influences_on_Adolescent_Materialism_A_New_Look_at_the_Role_of_Parents_and_Peers)

#### **2\. The Development of Market-Driven Identities in Young People: A Socio-Ecological Evolutionary Approach**

**Author:** Stephen Butler (2021)  
 **Journal:** Frontiers in Psychology, Vol. 12, Article 623675

**Abstract:** This paper hypothesizes that young people in advanced capitalism are obliged to develop an identity that is market-driven and embedded in self-narratives of success, status, and enhanced self-image. The prominence of extrinsic values in advanced capitalism are synergistic with neuro-maturational and stage-salient developments of adolescence and embodied in prominent market-driven criteria such as physical attractiveness, displays of wealth and material success, and high achievements. When young people show disproportionate extrinsic relative to intrinsic values, there is increased risk for mental health problems and poorer well-being.

**PDF Available:** [Full Text \- Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.623675/full) | [PMC/NIH](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8258256/)

#### **3\. Materialism, Conspicuous Consumption, and Brand Engagement in Self-Concept: A Study of Teenagers**

**Authors:** Justyna Kolańska-Stronka & Oleg Gorbaniuk (2022)  
 **Journal:** Current Issues in Personality Psychology, 10(1), 39–48

**Abstract:** This study examined 581 Polish adolescents (ages 13-17) to explore the relationships among materialism, conspicuous consumption, and brand engagement in self-concept. Higher materialism and conspicuous consumption were associated with higher brand engagement in self-concept, with conspicuous consumption acting as a significant mediator between materialism and brand engagement. The findings suggest that conspicuous consumption—using visible goods to display wealth or status—is partly driven by materialism and subsequently drives brand engagement.

**PDF Available:** [PMC/NIH](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10653352/) | [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/358774708_Materialism_conspicuous_consumption_and_brand_engagement_in_self-concept_a_study_of_teenagers)
